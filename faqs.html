Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about blood donation

Who can donate blood?

Anyone aged 18-65, in good health, and meeting donation requirements can donate blood. You must weigh at least 110 pounds and be free from certain medical conditions.

How to donate blood?

Visit a certified blood donation center or mobile drive, undergo a health screening, donate, and rest for a short period after donation. The entire process takes about 45-60 minutes.

How do I schedule an appointment?

You can schedule an appointment by visiting our website, calling a local blood donation center, or using our mobile app. Walk-ins are also welcome at most locations.

How often can I donate blood?

You can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks). For platelet donations, you can donate every 7 days, up to 24 times per year.

Is blood donation safe?

Yes, blood donation is very safe. All equipment used is sterile and disposable. Our medical staff are trained professionals who follow strict safety protocols.

What happens to my donated blood?

Your donated blood is tested for safety, processed into components (red cells, plasma, platelets), and distributed to hospitals to help patients in need.
